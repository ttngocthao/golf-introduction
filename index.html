Golf as we know it today originated from a game played on the eastern coast of Scotland in the Kingdom of Fife during the 15th century. Players would hit a pebble around a natural course of sand dunes, rabbit runs and tracks using a stick or primitive club. Read more

Object of the game

It is... to complete what is known as a hole by playing a ball from the teeing ground....into the hole on the putting green in the fewest number of strokes. A round of golf consists of playing 18 such holes.
Learning the game: If you are new to the game or thinking about playing, getting started can seem a little daunting, but it does not have to be. There is a pathway through from your first lessons to playing and then thinking about joining a golf club which you can take at your own pace.Read more...
GOLF EQUIPMENTS: encompasses the various items that are used to play the sport of golf. Types of equipment include the golf ball itself, implements designed for striking the golf ball, devices that aid in the process of playing a stroke, and items that in some way enrich the playing experience.

2. The stance:

All address your body, feet, knees, hips, forearms, shoulders and eyes should be positioned parallel to the target line Read more

3. Swinging a golf club

To build an efficient swing takes patience and lots of practice on a golf range. Once achieved, the fundamentals of the swing can carry over to most of the clubs in your bag. Read more

4. Weight shift

Proper weight shift throughout the swing is essential to promote distance and accuracy. Read more

5. Loft

Loft is the angle of the club face that controls trajectory and affects distance. Generally speaking, the higher number on your club means a higher ball flight and less distance. Read more

6. Course Management

Course management is the term used for making smart decisions as you work your way around the course, whether it is picking the right club for a certain shot, or picking a safe target line to keep your ball out of harms way. Read more

RULE: When playing golf, you must play the ball as it lies, whether your ball is in a good lie or a bad lie, unless the Rules allow you to do otherwise. ... At other times, you may incur a penalty if you wish to move your ball, e.g. when your ball is in a water hazard.
